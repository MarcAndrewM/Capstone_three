About

Motivation

As a high school distance running coach in the Bay Area, I frequently discuss college plans with the juniors and seniors. Since many have only visited a CSU or UC campus, they are unaware of all the other academic opportunities that span the United States. We try to visit all types of colleges throughout the state when we travel for running invitationals but I would like to expose the student-athletes to even more.

Design

The College Recommender uses 2018-2019 data from the National Center for Education Statistics. After scrubbing the data and placing it into a dataframe, I settled on various metrics to compare, which I deem relevant and understandable for a high schooler. The Recommender uses cosine similarity to evaluate schools and provides the top five "closest" schools in order of similarity.

Hope

I hope the student-athletes use this Recommender, stumble upon a school they might have never heard of, and be compelled to explore even more! By diversifying the schools they are considering, the student-athletes can more confidently decide which environment is ultimately the best fit for them.

Metrics

For more indepth explanations, please visit the National Center for Education Statistics.
When NCES did not have data for a certain metric, I used k-Nearest Neighbors with "k" equal to five to impute values, which allowed for cosine similarity analysis. At a high level, this used the five schools/("neighbors") that were most similar for the other metrics and then calculated a value for the unknown.
Average Net Price

Average net price for full-time, first-time degree/certificate-seeking undergraduates who were awarded grant or scholarship aid from federal, state or local governments, or the institution. Other sources of grant aid are excluded. Aid awarded anytime during the full aid year is included

Percent Admitted

Percent admitted total equals number of admissions-total(ADMSSN) divided by the total applicants(APPCLN)

Percent Women

Percent of student body that are women in the fall of the academic year. This variable is derived from the enrollment component that is collected in the winter and spring surveys.

Institution Size Cat

Institution size category based on total students enrolled for credit.

SAT Math 25th

The 25th percentile for first-time, degree/certificate-seeking undergraduate students.

SAT Verbal 25th

The 25th percentile for first-time, degree/certificate-seeking undergraduate students.
